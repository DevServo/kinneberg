Tips

From Our Family to Yours: Free Tips for Lawn Care

Mowing & Maintenance

  • Mow at a height of 3 inches or higher (helps shade out weeds).
  • Sharp blades are essential (dull blades tear and increase disease risk).
  • Leave clippings on the lawn (decomposing clippings improve soil quality).
  • Change mowing directions regularly.
  • Avoid mowing when the lawn is wet.
  • Follow the 1/3 rule: only cut up to 1/3 of the grass blade at a time.

Spring & Fall Care

  • Remove excessive debris to prevent suffocation and disease (like snow mold).
  • Maintain mowing height in fall (long grass protects from winter diseases).
  • (Spring) Remove rocks, sand, and salt from roadsides.
  • (Spring) Dethatch if necessary (hand rake or dethatching tool; avoid harsh sweepers).
  • Aerate your lawn (improves air circulation, reduces compaction, and promotes nutrient uptake).
  • (Fall) Overseed bare or thin areas.
  • Minimize foot traffic during winter to prevent damage to frozen grass.
